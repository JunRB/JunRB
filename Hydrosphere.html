Hydosphere

Different parts of the water cycle

The water moves from one reserve to another such as from a river to the ocean or from the ocean to the atmosphere
by the physical processes of evaporation, condensation, precipitation, infiltration, surface runoff, and subsurface
flow. In doing so, the water goes through different forms such as liquid, solid and gas/vapour.

The Water Cycle Explained

The water cycled when evaporations from the surface of the earth, rises into the atmosphere,
cools and condenses into rain or snow in clouds, and falls again to the surface as precipitation.
The water cycle is important because it is how water reaches plants, animals and us! Besides providing
people, animals and plants with water.
