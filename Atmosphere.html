The Carbon Cycle

Physical Processes:

Dead Living things get washed into rivers and streams, water currents take the carbon it contains around the oceans. The carbon can then settle to the ocean floor or to the ground.

Biological Process:

Respiration:

All living things respire talking in oxygen and exhaling carbon dioxide.

Photosynthesis:

All plants are producers which use sunlight and carbon dioxide to make sugar and oxygen

Decomposition and Decay:

The breaking down and decay of living organisms can create methane gas as a by-product of the bacteria or fungi that are eating the dead living organism.

Chemical Processes:

Combustion

When wood or other hydrocarbons like oil, gas or coal are burnt in oxygen they all produce carbon dioxide.

Dissolving into Carbonic Acid:

Cardon dioxide can react with water to form carbonic acid, this process can also reverse to release carbon dioxide from the ocean.

Limewater

Lime water is water with calcium hydroxide mixed

The carbon dioxide reacts with the water becoming carbonic acid.

The carbonic acid reacts with the calcium to form calcium carbonate
